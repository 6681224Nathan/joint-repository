## Undifferentaited marketing (Mass)
- **Pros**
	- Coveneince
- **Cons**
	- Inefficient : have to advertise everyone, high expense, instead of targeting at one group of people
	- ineffective : it's hard to serve all the people with one product, have to find the **common ground** and serve it, ==serve everyone, satisfy no one.
- What for?
	- **Small** market, and not competitive (Mass marketing works because it's just easy, marketing just like that, no need for deep divve when condition serves)
	- **Commodities** : dumb product, food, basic soap, salt, paper, oil
	- **New player** just enter the market, but after that, they can deep dive to target more specific customers

## Differentiated marketing (segmented)
More specific
- **Example** : Levi's, Toyota, **different models, for different customer segments**
- Example : Marriott international, a firm consists of thousand companies
	- Luxury, premium, select, longer stay, collector's
- **Pros** : 
	- **satisfy customer better (more specific)**, serve different segments differently, more specific food for everyone, tends to be satisfied
	- customers trade up and down between models during economic recession, from premium to budget model, all within the same brand
- **Cons** 
	- **cost** : production (lower number), distribution marketing bla bla bla while lower customer group, more expensive, **but still worth investing** 

## Concentrated marketing (niche)
Serves one or few small segments that are underserved by the current market
- **Example** : Shoes for vegan women
- **Why?? ** such small segment is pursued : because serving a large segment attracts competitors, but ==small segment can get a larger market share, **no one cares**==
- **Why02** : niche customers stay loyal, acheive strong market position, suits small firm with limited resource 
- **Cons** : hard for company to grow from that segment, acquired focused image that limits entry to other segments (ภาพจำ)
- Example : Amazon, specialized marketing, they are not just selling books
- Example : Hotel for overweight guest in Mehico

## Micromarketing
Involve both individual marketing and micro-marketing
- **Local marketing**
	- State level : North Carolina, a lot of old people so **best buy** have workshop for elderly in this state to teach them how to use IT product
	- Community level : Hispanic community in ___ city, they hire hispanic employees ... **Walmart**
	- Store/branch : **baskin robbins**, 3 miles radius rules, adjust product to suit those who live within 3 miles of the store
- **Individual marketing**
	Tailoring product to **individual**, like ==**eyeglasses**==, try to serve the individual characteristics
	- **Example : Roles Royce** let customers customize their cars to their **fullest extend**
	- Example : Personalized skin care system, using a machine mixer
	- One-to-one marketing : eyeglasses
	- ==Mass customization== : Salad bar, buffet, build-a-bear, barbie doll (customize hair skin color ...)
		- **Pros** : customization, good for me only
		- **Cons** : high production cost, worse quality control. **customization leads brand away from its identity**, try to be everyone, end up being no one
	- **Reason**
		- To use : tends to serve customer better, tend to build long-term relationship with customer, although it gets easier overtime to build specific product because of modern technology
		- To NOT use : customization gets brand's identity broken, brand's identity is lost

## Targeting strategy, choose which and which

- limited corporate resources : niche (specific customer, low player)
- product variability : differentiated (different models)
- product's life cycle, just enter : mass (one product, release first then get to know your customer later)
- market variability, market and customer varies in their needs and want : micro (individual) (needs for customization)
- competitor's marketing strategies, differentiated : mass (ignores segment difference, can't satisfy as good as differentiated)
